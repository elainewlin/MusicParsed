Your First Ukulele

Ukulele reviews

If you're looking to buy your first ukulele, I recommend a cheap (~$50) soprano from Amazon. It’s a low cost investment, and shipping is convenient. Don’t go too cheap (~$30) or else quality will suffer.

I’ve bought the following 2 ukes from Amazon:

  1. Donner soprano [recommended]
    1. Excellent value. Includes carrying case, strap, clip-on tuner, and extra strings.
    2. I usually keep this one at work. Let me know if you want to borrow it.
  2. Kala KA-15S soprano

I got a nicer ukulele after playing for about a year. I bought a Kala concert travel ($200) from Guitar Solo. Travel ukuleles are thinner, so it’s easy to pack in luggage. The material is better, and the uke has a crisper, louder sound.

Types of ukulele

If you’re starting out, there’s no need to worry too much about the particular wood, the brand, or the strings. The differences are subtle.

The biggest difference is size. There’s four main sizes: soprano, concert, tenor, and baritone. There’s also a sopranino, which is smaller than a soprano. Here are some more pictures of different ukulele sizes.


The sizes differ in tone and tuning. Larger ukuleles are louder because there’s more space for sound to resonate.

  • Generally, smaller ukuleles (sopranino, soprano, concert) come tuned high G, C, E, A.
  • Tenor ukuleles tend to have a low G.
  • Baritone ukuleles are tuned like the bottom 4 strings of a guitar: D, G, B, E.

However, by switching out what strings you use, you can customize the tuning. For a deeper base tone, you could replace the high G string on the soprano with a low G.
