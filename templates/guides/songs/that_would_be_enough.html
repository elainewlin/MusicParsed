Learn That Would Be Enough on ukulele

That Would Be Enough - Hamilton | Ukulele Play-Along

Start with an ukulele in standard tuning. I use an ukulele with a low G.

This guide includes the strum patterns I use to play That Would Be Enough from Hamilton. Click here for a free chord chart.

The finger-string assignment is:
  1. G - thumb
  2. C - index
  3. E - middle
  4. A - ring

For the first section with Eliza and Hamilton, most of the section is single strums. The section where Eliza sings "I'd knew you fight...to be alive right now." with the chords Bm, D, G, A uses the picking pattern: (1234)-1-(234)-1-(234)-1-(234)-1

For most of Eliza's section, the picking pattern I use is (1234)-2-(34)-2-(34)-2-(34)-2. When Eliza sings "When you decide to stay", on the word "stay" and the chord Bm, I switch to a strum pattern: 

  1+2+3+4+1+2+3+4+
  D  U U UD  U U U
